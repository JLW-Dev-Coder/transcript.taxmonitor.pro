IRS Code 670 Meaning

IRS Code 670 typically means a payment was received and posted to the tax module. What is IRS Code 670?

Plain-English definition: IRS Code 670 means the IRS posted a payment received to the account for that tax period.

Common transcript type
Account Transcript
Usually appears near
Codes 700, 706, 971
Typical context
Remittance posting, balance updates, interest/notice activity
Client-friendly summary
"A payment posted to your IRS account"

When does Code 670 appear?

  • After a taxpayer payment is processed: It posts when the IRS records the remittance to that module.
  • After a CP notice response or payment plan activity: You may see it around other collection or notice entries.
  • When payments are applied to a specific period: Confirm the tax period and the date to match the client’s proof of payment.

What does Code 670 usually signal?

If this appears alone

A payment posted, but the transcript view may not yet show the full downstream impact (recomputed balance, interest adjustments, or notice entries).

If this appears with interest codes (700/706)

The IRS may be assessing or reversing interest around the payment timing. Review dates and amounts to understand the net change to the balance.

If this appears with Code 971

A notice or miscellaneous entry was issued or posted. Pair the notice timing with the payment to understand what triggered the correspondence and whether follow-up is needed.

What should a tax professional do next?

  1. Match the payment to client proof: Confirm date, amount, and the tax period the payment applied to.
  2. Confirm application and net balance: Determine whether the payment reduced tax, penalties, interest, or was offset/adjusted.
  3. Document the sequence: Provide a short written summary (payment posted, what changed, what is still pending) and next steps.

FAQ

What does IRS Code 670 mean on a tax transcript?

IRS Code 670 generally indicates a payment posted to the tax module. It reflects that the IRS recorded a remittance for that period, which may reduce the balance due depending on how it was applied.

Is Code 670 the same as the IRS acknowledging a payment?

In most cases, yes. Code 670 is the transcript indicator that a payment was recorded. Confirm the date, amount, and tax period to ensure it matches the client’s payment proof.

Can Code 670 post without reducing the balance due?

Yes. A payment may be applied to penalties/interest first, offset to another module, returned, or later adjusted. Review subsequent activity to confirm the net effect.

What should I check next after Code 670 posts?

Verify the payment amount and application, then look for notices (often shown by Code 971), interest activity (700/706), and any holds that could affect resolution timing. Document the sequence in a client-ready report.
